Beat the Karachi heat: 12 pool maintenance tips for a crystal-clear summer.

Published July 2026 · 6 min read · By Unique Pools & Maintenance Co.

Karachi summers are unforgiving. With daytime temperatures regularly crossing 35°C and dust blowing in off the coast, an unsupervised pool can turn cloudy and green within days. The good news: a clear, healthy pool is mostly about routine, not expensive fixes. After three decades of building and servicing pools across Sindh, here are the twelve habits we recommend to every homeowner.

Tip 01

Run the pump long enough

In Karachi's summer, circulate the water 8–12 hours a day. Stagnant water is where algae starts. If your pool sees heavy use or sits in full sun, lean toward the higher end.

Tip 02

Test and balance chemistry weekly

Keep pH between 7.2 and 7.8, free chlorine around 1–3 ppm, and total alkalinity near 80–120 ppm. Out-of-balance water wastes chlorine and irritates skin. Test strips are cheap; a 10-minute check each weekend saves a green-pool rescue later.

Tip 03

Skim, brush, vacuum — in that order

Skim leaves and surface debris daily, brush the walls and floor weekly to dislodge biofilm, then vacuum. Brushing is the step most people skip, and it's the one that prevents stubborn staining.

Tip 04

Clean the filter on schedule

For sand and cartridge filters, backwash or rinse when the pressure gauge reads ~10 psi above its clean baseline. A clogged filter forces the pump to work harder and circulate less — raising both your bills and your algae risk.

Tip 05

Catch algae before it spreads

The first sign is usually a faint slippery feel on the walls or a cloudy tint. Hit it early with a shock dose and extra circulation. Prevention beats cure: keep sanitiser steady and never let the pump sit idle for days.

Tip 06

Watch the water level

Heat and wind drive off a surprising amount of water. Keep the level at the skimmer midpoint — too low and the pump can run dry; too high and the skimmer stops catching surface debris.

Tip 07

Cover it when it's idle

A pool cover cuts evaporation, keeps out dust, and slows chemical loss. Even a simple solar cover pays for itself in reduced top-ups and sanitiser over a single Karachi summer.

Tip 08

Manage the dust and the heat

Karachi's dust loads up filters fast and feeds algae. Rinse the deck and surrounding plants before dust settles in the water, and plan shade where you can — less direct sun means slower algae growth and lower chlorine burn-off.

Tip 09

Save energy with smart pump timing

Run the pump during off-peak hours and consider a variable-speed pump — it can cut filtration energy sharply versus a single-speed unit running all day. Lower running cost means you can circulate more, not less.

Tip 10

Service equipment before peak season

Have pumps, seals and automation checked in spring, before the May–August rush. A failing pump at the worst possible time is the most common cause of a lost summer.

Tip 11

Prep for monsoon and the off-season

Before heavy rains, clear overflow channels and debris screens so stormwater doesn't flood the pool with contaminants. In the quieter months, a reduced but consistent circulation schedule keeps equipment healthy.

Tip 12

Know when to call a pro

Persistent cloudiness, repeated algae, strange noises from the plant room, or rising bills all point to an underlying fault — usually filtration, plumbing or automation. Catching it early protects both the pool and your budget.

Quick answers

How often should I clean my pool in Karachi?

Skim daily in summer, brush and vacuum weekly, and test chemistry once a week. With a cover and steady circulation, most homeowners keep water clear with under two hours of hands-on work a week.

Why does my pool go green so fast?

Usually a combination of high heat, low circulation and unbalanced chlorine. Green algae thrives above 30°C when sanitiser burns off — steady pH and continuous pump time are the fix, not just more chemicals.

Can you maintain a pool I didn't build?

Yes. We service and rehabilitate pools across Karachi and Sindh regardless of who built them — from one-off clean-ups to ongoing maintenance contracts. More on our Karachi work.
